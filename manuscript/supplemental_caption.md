**Supplemental Figure X. Quality-control workflow for longitudinal aortic diameter analysis.** The pipeline begins with a long-format analytic dataset containing participant identifier, study date, height, aortic segment, diameter, and measurement source. After required-variable checks and date standardization, time since baseline is calculated for each participant. Segment-specific mixed-effects models are used to estimate expected aortic diameter and residuals. Measurements are flagged using residual-based and interval growth-rate criteria. All measurements are retained for auditability and patient-level plotting, whereas flagged measurements are excluded from downstream slope estimation and model-selection analyses. Final outputs include QC summaries, flagged-measurement files, patient-level slopes, progression categories, high-risk rankings based on observed-minus-expected diameter and aortic height index, and diagnostic plots.
